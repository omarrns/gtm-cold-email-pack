# Example: Growth leader email with stage match

**Frame:** `email-growth-leader`
**Recipient:** Head of Growth at an early-stage startup
**Connection:** None — recipient won't recognize the sender's last company. Stage match is the hook.

This is the kind of email the `email-growth-leader` skill produces when the target is a growth leader and the sender's edge is "I just built what you need built."

---

```
Hey [FirstName],

I just left [stage descriptor — e.g., "an enterprise AI startup, ~40 people"] — built the entire growth infrastructure from zero.

Quick context on me:
- Set up and wired the full stack — [tools matching the JD, e.g., HubSpot, Apollo, Salesforce, PostHog, Gong, Sendgrid] — and built the data flows between them.
- Built AI-powered outbound automation ([tools, e.g., Claude SDK, n8n, Exa, Firecrawl]) that went from nothing to [specific outcome — e.g., 2+ enterprise demos/week].
- AI-pilled (like a lot) — I build production systems, not just configure tools off the shelf.
- Former [revenue role], so I think about ops from the "does this actually help someone close a deal" side.

[Company] is at the exact stage I just came from — building foundational growth systems for the first time. That's where I'm strongest.

If this is relevant for the [role] role, I'd love to chat.

Best,
[FirstName]
```

---

## Why this works

- **Stage descriptor, not company name** — recipient won't recognize the company, so the descriptor does the work
- **Stack bullet matches the JD** — growth leaders scan tool lists for familiarity
- **Specific outcome** ("2+ enterprise demos/week") in the automation bullet
- **Identity bullet** differentiates from a tool-configurer
- **Bridge sentence** explicitly ties the stage match
- **Single ask, role-specific** — no menu of options
- **~120–130 words total**
