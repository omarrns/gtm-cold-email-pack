# Example: Founder email with a product tie

**Frame:** `email-founder` (product-user sub-frame)
**Recipient:** Co-founder/CEO of a devtools API company
**Connection:** Sender uses the API in production

This is the kind of email the `email-founder` skill produces when the target is a founder, the sender has a real product connection, and there's a specific, observable gap.

---

```
Subject: interested in working at [Company].

[FirstName], I figured I'd reach out directly as I'm interested in working at [Company].

Quick context on me:
- I'm a [current role descriptor] and I've been using [Company]'s API in prod (like a lot) — built it into our [specific use case].
- I build with these tools daily: [tool list, including target's product].
- Been on the [thesis one-liner] problem since [origin point].

Genuinely love [Company]'s product, which is why it bugs me when [Competitor] is defining your positioning when Googling "[Company] alternatives". Zero enterprise case studies on the site, and you just raised at $[X]M. That's a fixable gap with content engineering and internal systems.

At [SenderLastCompany] I solved this with [HeroProject] — turned [unstructured input] into [structured output that maps to the gap].

Would love to chat if growth/marketing hiring is on your radar.

Hope to hear back,
[FirstName]
```

---

## Why this works

- **Product user credibility** in bullet 1 (instant trust signal)
- **Specific gap** with a competitor name, search query, and funding contrast (not "your content could be better")
- **Hero achievement bridge** that maps to the gap, not a generic resume bullet
- **Conditional ask** ("if … is on your radar") removes pressure
- **~150 words total** — short enough to read in one breath
