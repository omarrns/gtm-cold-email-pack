# Example: Hiring manager email with JD anchor

**Frame:** `email-hiring-manager`
**Recipient:** Hiring manager or recruiter for a specific role
**Connection:** None required — the JD itself is the anchor.

This is the kind of email the `email-hiring-manager` skill produces when the user has a JD in hand and wants the email anchored on JD outcomes (not responsibilities).

---

```
Subject: [Role title] — already shipped this

Hey [FirstName],

Saw the [Role] role at [Company]. I [current status from sender-profile.md] — read the JD and three things lined up:

- JD wants: [paraphrased outcome 1, e.g., "build a programmatic content pipeline"]. I [hero achievement from sender-profile.md that maps to outcome 1, with a real metric].
- JD wants: [paraphrased outcome 2, e.g., "stand up the marketing tech stack"]. I [hero achievement that maps to outcome 2, with the stack named].
- JD wants: [paraphrased outcome 3, e.g., "ship competitive intelligence content"]. I [hero achievement that maps to outcome 3, with a concrete output].

The one stretch is [genuine gap, e.g., "I haven't owned a paid budget at scale"], but [compensating angle — e.g., "I'd be partnering with your existing paid ops lead, and the org chart shows that's the structure"].

Worth a 20-minute conversation? Happy to walk through [specific outcome from your hero achievements] in detail.

Thanks,
[FirstName]
```

---

## Why this works

- **Outcomes, not responsibilities** — each bullet quotes what the JD wants done and pairs it with proof the sender has done that exact thing
- **Three bullets, not five** — discipline beats volume
- **Honest gap** preempts the "are they being real about the gap?" question
- **Single ask with specificity** — "walk through [outcome]" gives the hiring manager a reason to say yes
- **~110–130 words total**
